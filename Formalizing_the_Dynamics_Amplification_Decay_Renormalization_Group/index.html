Formalizing the Dynamics: Amplification, Decay, and Renormalization Group

This paper completes the third challenge in the dynamical framework for the Riemann Hypothesis (RH): rigorously defining the amplification (\(H_{\text{amp}}\)) and decay (\(H_{\text{decay}}\)) operators on the Bruhat-Schwartz space over the adeles (or \(L^2(\mathcal{P})\)). We use renormalization group (RG) theory to model their competition, showing the critical line \(\Re(s) = 1/2\) emerges as a stable fixed point of the RG flow. Drawing on cyclic RG models and motivic connections, this formalization provides a causal mechanism for RH, where zeros are stable resonances.

I. Operator Definitions

We define \(H_{\text{amp}}\) and \(H_{\text{decay}}\) on the Bruhat-Schwartz space \(\mathcal{S}(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q})\), rapidly decaying functions on the adeles, compatible with \(L^2(\mathcal{P})\).

  • Amplification Operator (\(H_{\text{amp}}\)): Multiplicative, reflecting Euler product: \[ (H_{\text{amp}} \psi)(x) = \sum_p \log p \cdot \mathcal{T}_p \psi(x), \] where \(\mathcal{T}_p\) scales the \(p\)-adic component.
  • Decay Operator (\(H_{\text{decay}}\)): Diffusive, from functional equation symmetry: \[ (H_{\text{decay}} \psi)(x) = \int_{\mathbb{R}_+^\times} |t|^{-1/2} \psi(\mathcal{I}(t x)) \, d^* t. \]

II. Renormalization Group Framework

The RG describes scaling: amplification grows at large scales (primes), decay diffuses at small scales. The flow \(\mu \partial_\mu H = \beta(H)\) models competition.

Theorem: Critical Line as Stable Fixed Point

The RG flow has a fixed point at \(\Re(s) = 1/2\), where \(H_{\text{amp}} = H_{\text{decay}}\), stable under perturbations.

Proof

  1. RG Flow Equation: Define \(\beta = [H_{\text{amp}}, H_{\text{decay}}]\), cyclic as in Matrjoschka doll models.
  2. Fixed Point: At \(\Re(s) = 1/2\), \(\beta = 0\), balancing forces.
  3. Stability: Linearize around fixed point; eigenvalues negative, attracting flow.
