Conjectural Proof of the Riemann Hypothesis via Dynamical Stability and RG Flow

This paper attempts a full, conjectural proof of the Riemann Hypothesis (RH) based on the dynamical framework developed in prior works. Building on the Primal Manifold \(\mathcal{P}\), the kernel \(K(x,y)\), and the trace formula, we show that non-trivial zeros must lie on \(\Re(s) = 1/2\) as the only stable fixed point of the renormalization group (RG) flow. This relies on unproven but plausible assumptions from quantum chaos and RG theory (e.g., cyclic RG models). We then provide numerical verification of low-lying zeros and prime counts under RH.

I. Conjectural Proof

Theorem: Riemann Hypothesis (Conjectural)

All non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\) have \(\Re(s) = 1/2\).

Proof (Conjectural)

  1. Stability Condition: From prior, zeros are stable eigenstates where \(H_{\text{amp}} \psi = H_{\text{decay}} \psi\).
  2. RG Flow: The beta function \(\beta = [H_{\text{amp}}, H_{\text{decay}}]\) has fixed point at \(\Re(s) = 1/2\), stable per linearization (negative eigenvalues).
  3. Off-Line Instability: Perturbations away from 1/2 lead to divergence (amplification dominates) or collapse (decay dominates), precluding zeros.
  4. Conclusion: Assuming RG captures the scaling, RH holds.
